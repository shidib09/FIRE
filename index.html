The Big Picture

Should we pay off the house or invest?

Most people think this is an either/or question. It's not. It's a question of order.

```
😰

The normal way

Pay the minimum on the mortgage for 30 years while trying to invest on the side. You're paying interest and hoping the market cooperates — for three decades.

Risky. Stressful. Long.
💡

Our way

Crush the mortgage first, then redirect every dollar that was going to the bank — into our own future. By the time we invest, we have zero debt and maximum firepower.

Strategic. Calm. Certain.
The key insight

Paying off a 5.25% mortgage is the same as earning a guaranteed 5.25% return — tax-free, risk-free, every year. And no market can promise that consistently.

```

The Math, Simply

What the numbers actually say

At our mortgage rate, paying off first then investing actually comes out ahead — or very close. And it comes with something the other path can't offer.

```
3.5% rate
Invest first wins

At a very low rate, the stock market's long-run average easily beats the cost of keeping the mortgage. Investing while holding the debt makes sense here.

6.0% rate
It's basically a toss-up

Investing technically wins by ~$72K on paper — but that assumes 30 uninterrupted years of 7% returns. One bad decade wipes that advantage out.

5.25% rate ← Us
Too close to call

At 5.25%, the math is genuinely close — but paying off still gives a guaranteed return, zero risk, and full ownership. When our halal fund options underperform broad market averages, paying off wins clearly.

The thing math can't measure
```
